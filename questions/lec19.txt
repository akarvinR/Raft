1. What is Chord? (Use a single sentence)

Chord is a distributed lookup protocol that efficiently locates the node that stores a particular data item.

2. What is the problem Chord addresses and why is it important? (Use no more than two sentences)

Chord addresses the problem of efficiently locating the node that stores a particular data item.
Efficient lookup is important for maintaining low latency, and response time, and for providing good under experience.
And Chord ensures that a query is answered within a bounded number of hops.

3. What is the core idea in Chord that differentiates it from prior work on DHTs?
Chord maps keys onto nodes.

Traditional services provide a direct mapping between keys and values.
